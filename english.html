About

Not a large agency.
Just the agent himself.

I'm Maarten Eshuis — born and raised in Amsterdam, VastgoedCert registered buying agent and active in the Amsterdam property market since the 1990s. Over that time I have guided hundreds of purchase and sale transactions, coordinated renovation projects and helped owners make their homes more sustainable.

What sets me apart? I have a structural and mechanical engineering background. That means I don't just look at price — I assess construction quality, technical condition, sustainability potential and the legal context. Knowledge most agents simply don't possess.

I work for a deliberately small group of clients — guaranteeing exclusive, personal attention throughout. You call me, not a receptionist. You speak to me, not a junior employee.

For Expats & International Buyers

New to the Dutch
housing market?

Amsterdam's property market is one of the most competitive in Europe — and one of the most procedurally complex. As an international buyer, the risks of navigating it alone are significant: overbidding, hidden defects, unfamiliar legal requirements and missed red flags in zoning plans.

I guide international clients through every step, entirely in English — from the first search to the signing at the notary.

As early as possible — ideally before your first viewing. The sooner I'm involved, the better I can shape your search and filter the right properties. Even if you already have a property in mind, I can step in quickly.
The selling agent represents the seller's interests. As your buying agent I am exclusively on your side — the buyer. I negotiate for you, not the other party. In Amsterdam's competitive market, that distinction is critical.
Amsterdam has a lot of older housing stock. Hidden defects, foundation issues, asbestos, or a property that can barely be made sustainable — these are costly surprises. My engineering background lets me assess this on the spot, without additional costs for an external surveyor.
A VvE (Vereniging van Eigenaren) is the homeowners association for an apartment building. It manages shared maintenance, finances and reserves. A poorly run VvE can mean large unexpected costs. I review all VvE documents carefully before you make an offer.
